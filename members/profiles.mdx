Member profiles are the foundation of ChurchOS. Every person in your congregation — whether a long-standing member, a first-time visitor, or a child in your youth program — gets a dedicated profile that stores their contact details, family connections, group memberships, and any custom data your church needs to track.

## Adding a Member

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Add Member form">
    From your Dashboard, navigate to **Members** in the left sidebar, then click **Add Member** in the top-right corner.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in contact information">
    Enter the person's **first name**, **last name**, **email address**, **phone number**, **home address**, and **birthday**. All fields except first and last name are optional at this stage and can be added later.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the member status">
    Choose one of the four statuses that best describes this person's relationship to your church:
    - **Active** — a current, participating member
    - **Inactive** — a member who is no longer regularly attending
    - **Visitor** — someone attending services but not yet a member
    - **Guest** — a one-time or occasional attendee
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the profile">
    Click **Save Profile**. The profile is created immediately and appears in your Members list. You can return at any time to add more details, upload a photo, or link family members.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Custom Fields

Admins can extend every member profile with fields that are specific to your church. To set these up, go to **Settings > Custom Fields** and create fields of the following types:

| Field Type | Best Used For |
|---|---|
| **Text** | Notes, membership numbers, or any free-form entry |
| **Date** | Baptism date, membership anniversary, salvation date |
| **Dropdown** | Campus location, ministry interest, life stage |
| **Checkbox** | Volunteer consent, newsletter opt-in, background check |

**Examples of popular custom fields:**
- *Baptism Date* — tracks when a member was baptized
- *Ministry Interest* — a dropdown listing areas like Worship, Outreach, or Children's Ministry
- *Campus* — assigns the member to a specific campus for multi-site churches

Once created, custom fields appear on every member profile and can be used as filters on the Members list.

## Family Records

Linking family members together makes it easy to see household relationships and send family-level communications. To link a family member to a profile:

1. Open the member's profile and scroll to the **Family** section.
2. Click **Add Family Member**.
3. Search for an existing member by name or email, or create a new profile on the spot.
4. Select the relationship type (Spouse, Child, Parent, Sibling, Other) and click **Save**.

Linked family members appear as a group on each individual's profile, and you can view the full household by clicking **View Family Unit**.

## Bulk Import via CSV

If you are migrating from another system or have member data in a spreadsheet, you can import everyone at once using a CSV file.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Download the CSV template">
    In the Members list, click **Import**, then select **Download Template**. This gives you a pre-formatted CSV with all supported column headers.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in your member data">
    Open the template in a spreadsheet application and populate it with your member records. Each row represents one person. Required columns are `first_name` and `last_name`; all others are optional.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload your completed CSV">
    Return to **Members > Import** and click **Upload CSV**. Select your completed file from your computer.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Map your columns">
    ChurchOS will attempt to auto-match your column headers to profile fields. Review each mapping and correct any mismatches before proceeding.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm the import">
    Review the preview summary showing the number of records to be created. Click **Confirm Import** to finish. A confirmation email is sent to your admin address when the import is complete.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Editing and Archiving Members

To edit a profile, open it from the Members list and click **Edit** in the top-right corner. Make your changes and click **Save**.

To archive a member, open their profile, click the **Actions** menu (⋯), and select **Archive Member**. Archived members are removed from your active Members list but remain fully searchable by enabling the **Show Archived** toggle.

<Note>
  Archiving a member does **not** delete their record. All profile data, group history, and custom field values are preserved. You can restore an archived member at any time by opening their profile and clicking **Restore Member**.
</Note>
