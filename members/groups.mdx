Groups in ChurchOS let you organize your congregation into meaningful communities — small groups meeting in homes, ministry teams serving on Sunday mornings, adult education classes, or governance committees like a deacon board. Every group gets its own roster, leader, and communication tools so you can engage each segment of your church without leaving the platform.

## Creating a Group

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the New Group form">
    From the Members module, click **Groups** in the top navigation, then select **New Group**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name your group">
    Give the group a clear, descriptive name. Examples include *Youth Ministry*, *Choir*, *Deacon Board*, *Wednesday Night Bible Study*, or *Campus Serve Team*. The name is visible to group members and leaders in the member portal.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the group type">
    Choose the type that best describes this group:
    - **Small Group** — intimate, relationship-focused gatherings
    - **Ministry** — teams that serve a specific function in the church
    - **Class** — ongoing or short-term educational groups
    - **Other** — committees, boards, or anything that doesn't fit the above
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a group leader">
    Search for an existing member to serve as the group leader. Leaders can view the group roster, send messages to the group, and (if granted permission) add or remove members. You can assign multiple leaders to a single group.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the group">
    Click **Save Group**. The group is created and appears in your Groups list. You can start adding members immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Adding Members to a Group

You have two ways to populate a group with members:

**Manual addition:** Open the group, click **Add Members**, and search for individuals by name or email. Select each person and click **Add to Group**.

**Bulk assignment via filters:** In the main Members list, use the **Filter** toolbar to narrow down your list by status, custom field, campus, or any other criteria. Once you have the right segment, select all filtered members and choose **Assign to Group** from the bulk actions menu. Select your target group and confirm.

## Group Communication

You can send a message to every member of a group directly from the group page. Open the group, click **Send Message**, compose your email or SMS, and click **Send**. ChurchOS delivers the message to each group member's preferred contact method on file. Message history is stored on the group page for reference.

## Group Types

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Small Groups">
    Small groups are designed for community and discipleship. They typically meet regularly — weekly or bi-weekly — in homes or church facilities. In ChurchOS, small groups can have a designated meeting day, time, and location visible to members in the portal, making it easy for new attendees to find and join a group that fits their schedule.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Ministry Teams">
    Ministry teams are functional serving groups within the church — worship team, AV crew, hospitality, children's check-in, and so on. Ministry groups often have roster-level needs like role assignments and background check tracking. Use custom fields at the group level to capture this information alongside each member's individual profile.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Classes">
    Classes support ongoing or short-term educational programming — Sunday school, membership classes, financial stewardship courses, and more. You can set a start and end date on a class group to indicate its intended duration. After the class ends, the group can be archived while preserving the roster history.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Tip>
  For large ministries with distinct sub-teams — for example, a Worship Ministry that includes separate Vocals, Band, and Tech crews — create a parent group for the ministry and individual sub-groups for each team. Link the sub-groups to the parent by noting the parent group name in the group description, or use a **Campus** or **Team** custom field on member profiles to filter across all layers at once.
</Tip>
