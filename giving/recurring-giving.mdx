Recurring giving is one of the most powerful tools your church has for building a stable financial foundation. When donors commit to an automated giving schedule, your ministry can plan budgets, fund long-term projects, and invest in your congregation with confidence. ChurchOS makes it easy for members to set up recurring gifts and gives your admin team full visibility into every active schedule — so you're always in the loop, even when giving happens automatically in the background.

## How Members Set Up Recurring Giving

Donors set up a recurring schedule directly from your church's giving page — no separate registration or portal required. During the giving flow, a donor:

1. Enters a donation amount and selects a fund.
2. Chooses a giving frequency: **Weekly**, **Bi-Weekly**, or **Monthly**.
3. Selects a start date for the first automated payment.
4. Completes their payment method details and confirms the schedule.

The donor receives a confirmation email with the schedule details and a link to manage their recurring gift at any time. Subsequent payments are processed automatically on the chosen schedule until the donor pauses or cancels.

## Admin View: Managing All Recurring Schedules

Navigate to **Giving > Recurring** to see a complete list of every active recurring giving schedule in your church. For each schedule you can see:

- **Donor name and contact info**
- **Gift amount and designated fund**
- **Giving frequency** (weekly, bi-weekly, or monthly)
- **Next payment date**
- **Schedule status** (active, paused, or cancelled)

You can search or filter by donor name, fund, or status to quickly find the schedule you're looking for.

## Pausing, Cancelling, or Editing a Schedule

As an admin, you can manage any recurring schedule on behalf of a member. From **Giving > Recurring**, click the **⋯** menu next to any schedule to:

- **Pause** — temporarily suspend automatic payments. The schedule remains in place and can be resumed at any time without the donor needing to re-enter their payment details.
- **Cancel** — permanently end the recurring schedule. The donor will receive an email notification confirming the cancellation.
- **Edit** — update the gift amount, fund designation, or payment frequency. Changes take effect on the next scheduled payment date.

Donors can also manage their own schedules at any time by visiting your giving page and logging into their donor profile.

## Failed Payments

If a recurring payment fails — due to an expired card, insufficient funds, or another processor-level issue — ChurchOS takes the following steps automatically:

1. **Donor notification** — an email is sent to the donor immediately, prompting them to update their payment method via a secure link.
2. **Admin notification** — a failed payment alert appears in your **Giving > Recurring** dashboard and is sent to your admin email so you can follow up personally if needed.
3. **Retry logic** — your connected payment processor (Stripe or PayPal) will attempt to retry the payment according to its standard retry schedule.

Addressing failed payments promptly helps preserve your donor relationships and keeps your giving totals accurate.

## Pledge Management

ChurchOS supports pledge campaigns alongside recurring giving schedules. To set up a pledge campaign:

1. Go to **Giving > Pledges** and click **New Campaign**.
2. Name the campaign (e.g., "Building Fund Capital Campaign"), set a goal amount, and define the pledge period.
3. Share the pledge form link with your congregation so members can commit to a giving goal.
4. Track fulfillment in **Giving > Pledges** — see each donor's pledged amount, total paid to date, and remaining balance at a glance.

Pledge campaigns can be linked to a specific giving fund so that designated gifts automatically count toward fulfillment.

<Tip>
  Send your congregation a direct link to your recurring giving page — rather than your general giving page — to make it as easy as possible for donors to commit to a schedule. You can find this URL under **Giving > Settings > Giving Link > Recurring Giving Direct Link**.
</Tip>
