At the end of every tax year, your donors depend on your church to provide an accurate record of their contributions. ChurchOS makes it straightforward to generate, review, and distribute year-end contribution statements that meet IRS requirements — covering every gift recorded in your system for the selected year. Whether you email statements directly from ChurchOS or download a ZIP of PDFs to print and mail, the entire process can be completed in a matter of minutes.

## Generating Tax Statements

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Tax Statements">
    Go to **Giving > Tax Statements** in your ChurchOS admin panel. This section is only accessible to users with the **Finance Admin** or **Administrator** role.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the Tax Year">
    Use the **Tax Year** dropdown to choose the year for which you want to generate statements. ChurchOS will pull all recorded donations within that calendar year (January 1 – December 31) for every donor in your system.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review Contributions Per Donor">
    Before generating statements, review the summary table that lists each donor alongside their total contributions for the year. Verify that totals look accurate and check for any donors who may need to be merged or have missing contact information. You can click any donor's name to see a detailed transaction breakdown.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Customize the Statement Header">
    Click **Statement Settings** to configure the information that will appear at the top of every statement:
    - **Church name** — as you want it to appear on official documents
    - **EIN (Employer Identification Number)** — your church's federal tax ID
    - **Church address** — the mailing address of your organization
    - **Custom message** — an optional thank-you note or scripture to personalize the statement
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate PDF Statements">
    Click **Generate Statements**. ChurchOS will compile an individually formatted PDF for every donor who made at least one contribution during the selected year. Generation typically takes less than a minute, depending on the size of your donor list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Email or Download Statements">
    Once generation is complete, you have two distribution options:
    - **Email directly** — click **Send All** to email each donor's statement to the address on file in ChurchOS. You can also send to individual donors by clicking **Send** next to their name.
    - **Download ZIP** — click **Download All (ZIP)** to save a compressed folder of all PDF statements to your computer for printing and physical mailing.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What's Included in Each Statement

Every ChurchOS tax statement includes the following information:

- **Donor name and mailing address**
- **Itemized giving history** — each contribution listed with its date, amount, and fund designation (e.g., General Fund, Building Fund, Missions)
- **Total contributions** for the tax year
- **Church name, EIN, and address**
- **IRS compliance language** — a standard statement confirming no goods or services were provided in exchange for the donation (required for contributions to be tax-deductible), which you should review to confirm it accurately reflects your church's practices

## Emailing vs. Printing Statements

**Emailing** is the fastest and most cost-effective distribution method. Statements arrive in donors' inboxes within minutes, and ChurchOS logs a delivery record for each email sent. Donors who do not have an email address on file will be flagged so you can follow up manually.

**Printing** is the right choice for donors who prefer a physical copy or who don't have a valid email address on file. Download the ZIP file, print the PDFs, and mail them to the addresses listed on each statement. Consider printing on your church's letterhead for a more personal touch.

You can also do a combination of both — email those with addresses on file and print the remainder.

<Warning>
  Before generating and sending statements, verify that your church's **EIN is correct** in **Giving > Tax Statements > Statement Settings**. Statements sent with an incorrect EIN may be invalid for tax purposes and could require you to reissue corrected documents to all donors. Double-check this detail every year, especially if your church has recently updated its federal tax registration.
</Warning>
