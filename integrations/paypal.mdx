PayPal is a trusted alternative to Stripe for churches that want to offer their congregation a familiar, widely-used payment option. It's especially well-suited for mobile giving and for reaching donors who prefer to give without entering a card number directly. If your church has international members or partners, PayPal's global reach makes it a strong choice.

## What PayPal Enables

Connecting PayPal to ChurchOS gives your congregation the following giving options:

- **One-time donations** — Donors can give instantly using their PayPal balance, linked bank account, or credit card.
- **Mobile-friendly giving** — PayPal's checkout is optimized for smartphones, making it easy to give from anywhere.
- **Global donor access** — Accept gifts from donors in over 200 countries and regions in multiple currencies.

## Connecting PayPal

<Note>
  A **PayPal Business account** is required to connect PayPal to ChurchOS. Personal PayPal accounts are not supported. You can create or upgrade to a Business account for free at [paypal.com](https://www.paypal.com/us/business).
</Note>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the PayPal integration page">
    In your ChurchOS dashboard, go to **Settings** → **Integrations** → **PayPal**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start the connection">
    Click **Connect PayPal**. You'll be redirected to PayPal's secure authorization page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sign in with your PayPal Business account">
    Enter your PayPal Business account credentials and log in. If you don't have a Business account yet, you can create one directly from this screen.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Authorize ChurchOS">
    Review the permissions being requested and click **Agree and Connect**. This grants ChurchOS the ability to process payments on behalf of your account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="PayPal giving is live">
    After authorization, you'll be returned to ChurchOS. PayPal now appears as a giving option on your church's giving page.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Processing Fees

PayPal's standard processing fees apply to all transactions. Eligible nonprofits may qualify for significantly reduced rates through PayPal Giving Fund.

| Fee Type | Standard Rate | Nonprofit Rate (PayPal Giving Fund) |
|---|---|---|
| Card or PayPal balance transaction | ~3.49% + 49¢ | 1.99% + 49¢ |
| ChurchOS platform fee | $0 | $0 |

<Tip>
  If your church is a registered 501(c)(3), apply for PayPal's nonprofit discount through [PayPal Giving Fund](https://www.paypalgiving.org). Approved organizations pay just **1.99% + 49¢** per transaction — a meaningful savings for high-volume giving periods like year-end campaigns.
</Tip>

## Stripe vs. PayPal — Quick Comparison

Both payment processors work seamlessly within ChurchOS. Here's a side-by-side comparison to help you decide which is right for your church — or whether you'd like to offer both.

| Feature | Stripe | PayPal |
|---|---|---|
| Setup | Free, ~5 minutes | Free, ~5 minutes |
| Standard fee | 2.9% + 30¢ | ~3.49% + 49¢ |
| Nonprofit discount | Not available | Yes, via PayPal Giving Fund (1.99% + 49¢) |
| Recurring giving | ✅ Fully supported | ⚠️ Limited support |
| Text-to-give | ✅ Yes | ❌ No |
| Global reach | 46 countries | 200+ countries |
| Donor familiarity | High | Very high |

Many churches choose to enable **both** Stripe and PayPal so donors can give using whichever method they prefer.
