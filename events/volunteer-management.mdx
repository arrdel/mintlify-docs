ChurchOS Volunteer Management helps you move from scattered group texts and manual sign-up sheets to a streamlined, automated scheduling system. You can define every role in your church, build schedules for upcoming services, send confirmation requests to volunteers, and let ChurchOS handle the reminder follow-ups — so you can focus on ministry instead of logistics.

## Setting up volunteer roles

Before you start scheduling, define the roles that exist in your church. Roles represent the positions you need filled each service, such as Greeter, Usher, Sound Technician, or Kids Teacher.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Volunteer Roles">
    Navigate to **Events → Volunteers → Roles** and click **Add Role**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Define the role">
    Enter a **Role Name** (for example, "Greeter," "Usher," "Kids Teacher — Nursery," or "Sound Tech"), an optional description, and the **ministry area** this role belongs to (Worship, Kids, Hospitality, Tech, and so on).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the required count">
    In the **Required Per Service** field, enter how many volunteers are needed to fill this role at a typical service. ChurchOS uses this number to flag when a service is under-staffed during scheduling.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the role">
    Click **Save Role**. The role is now available when building service schedules and is visible to volunteers in the member portal.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Scheduling volunteers

Once your roles are set up, you can assign volunteers to specific services from the schedule builder.

1. Go to **Events → Volunteers → Schedule** and select the service date you want to staff.
2. Click **Add Volunteer** next to any role to search your member database and assign a person.
3. Repeat for each role until the service is fully staffed. Roles that are under-staffed are highlighted in amber.
4. When the schedule is ready, click **Send Confirmations** to email each volunteer a request confirming their availability. Volunteers can respond with **Accept** or **Decline** directly from the email.
5. Track RSVP status in the **Schedule** view — each volunteer shows a badge of **Confirmed**, **Declined**, or **Pending**.

## Volunteer notifications and reminders

ChurchOS sends automated notifications to keep volunteers informed without requiring manual follow-up from you.

- **Confirmation request** — sent immediately when you assign a volunteer to a service and click Send Confirmations.
- **Reminder notification** — sent automatically 48 hours before the scheduled service to every confirmed volunteer.
- **Schedule change alert** — sent if you reassign or remove a volunteer from a service after they have already confirmed.

<Note>
  Volunteers receive an automatic reminder notification 48 hours before their scheduled service. Make sure each volunteer's email address is current in their member profile so these reminders reach them reliably.
</Note>

## Managing volunteer availability

Volunteers can tell ChurchOS in advance when they are unavailable — for vacations, travel, or other commitments — so you don't accidentally schedule them when they can't serve.

- **Mobile app:** Volunteers open **My Schedule → Unavailability → Add Dates** and select the date range they'll be away.
- **Member portal:** Volunteers log in at your church's member portal URL, go to **My Volunteering → Availability**, and block out dates.

When you open the schedule builder, ChurchOS automatically greys out any volunteer who has marked themselves unavailable for that service date, preventing accidental double-booking.

## Handling substitution requests

When a confirmed volunteer cancels, ChurchOS makes it straightforward to find a replacement:

1. Click the volunteer's name in the schedule and select **Mark as Cancelled**.
2. ChurchOS immediately surfaces a list of **eligible substitutes** — members with the same role who are available and have not yet been scheduled for that service.
3. Select a substitute from the list and click **Send Request**. The substitute receives a targeted email explaining the open slot and can accept or decline with one click.
4. Once they accept, the schedule updates automatically and both the original volunteer and the substitute receive a confirmation.

## Volunteer history report

To view a complete record of every service a volunteer has served in, go to **Events → Volunteers → Reports → Volunteer History**. You can filter by:

- **Individual volunteer** — see every role and service date for a specific person.
- **Role** — see everyone who has served in a particular role over a date range.
- **Date range** — export a summary of all volunteer activity for a given month or season.

Use this report to identify your most consistent volunteers, recognise long-term service, and spot roles that are chronically under-staffed.
