The Prayer Requests feature in ChurchOS gives your congregation a safe and accessible way to share their needs and receive spiritual support. Whether a member submits a request through the mobile app on their way to work or a visitor fills out a form on your website, every request flows into a centralized prayer board where your team can respond with care, assign follow-ups, and track answered prayers.

## How Members Submit Prayer Requests

Members and visitors can submit prayer requests through three channels:

- **Mobile App** — members who have downloaded the ChurchOS app can tap **Prayer Requests > Submit a Request** from the home screen at any time.
- **Member Portal** — members can log in to the web-based member portal at your church's ChurchOS URL and submit a request from the **Prayer** section.
- **Public Submission Form** — ChurchOS generates a shareable, publicly accessible submission form link that you can post on your church website, in a bulletin, or on social media. No login is required to submit through this form. Find the link under **Communication > Prayer Requests > Share Form Link**.

## Managing the Prayer Board

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Prayer Board">
    From the left navigation, go to **Communication > Prayer Requests**. You'll see all incoming requests listed in chronological order, with the submitter's name (or "Anonymous"), the date submitted, and a preview of the request.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review Incoming Requests">
    Click any request to open the full details. You'll see the complete request text, submission channel, privacy setting, and any previous activity (responses sent, team members assigned, status changes).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Update the Request Status">
    Use the **Status** dropdown to keep your prayer board organized:
    - **Praying** — your team is actively praying for this request
    - **Answered** — the member has reported or you have noted that this prayer was answered
    - **Archived** — the request is no longer active but is retained for records
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign to a Prayer Team Member">
    Click **Assign** and select a prayer team member from the dropdown. The assigned team member will receive a notification (via email or the ChurchOS app) and can view the request in their personal **My Assignments** queue.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send a Response to the Requester">
    Click **Send Response** to compose a personal message to the person who submitted the request. You can send this via email or SMS. Let them know their request has been received, that your team is praying, and offer any pastoral follow-up. Responses are logged in the request's activity timeline.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Prayer Team Access

You can designate specific members of your staff or volunteer team as **Prayer Team Members**. Prayer team members can:

- View and manage requests assigned to them
- Update request statuses and add notes
- Send responses to requesters
- See requests that have been shared with the prayer team (based on privacy settings)

To assign someone to the prayer team, go to **Settings > Roles & Permissions > Prayer Team** and add their ChurchOS user account. Prayer team members do not need full admin access — their permissions are scoped specifically to prayer request management.

## Privacy Settings

Every prayer request has a privacy level that controls who on your team can see it. Submitters can choose their preferred privacy level when submitting, and admins can adjust the setting at any time.

| Privacy Level       | Who Can See the Request                          |
|---------------------|--------------------------------------------------|
| **Private**         | Admins only — not visible to prayer team members |
| **Prayer Team**     | Admins and assigned prayer team members          |
| **Public (Prayer Wall)** | Admins, prayer team, and displayed on the public prayer wall (if enabled) |

When submitting through the public form, the default privacy level is **Prayer Team**. You can change the default under **Communication > Prayer Requests > Settings**.

## Public Prayer Wall

ChurchOS can display approved prayer requests on a public-facing prayer wall embedded in your church website. This encourages congregation-wide intercession and builds a sense of community.

To enable the prayer wall:

1. Go to **Communication > Prayer Requests > Settings > Public Prayer Wall**.
2. Toggle **Enable Prayer Wall** to on.
3. Copy the embed code or shareable URL and add it to your church website.

Only requests explicitly marked as **Public** will appear on the prayer wall. Submitters' last names are hidden by default on the public wall — only first names are shown. You can choose to display requests anonymously for all public wall entries under the prayer wall settings.

<Note>
  All prayer requests in ChurchOS are treated as confidential by default. Requests are only visible to admins unless explicitly shared with the prayer team or marked as public. Encourage your congregation to submit requests freely, knowing their needs are handled with discretion and care.
</Note>
