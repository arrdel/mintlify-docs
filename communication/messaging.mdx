ChurchOS Messaging lets you reach your congregation at scale — whether you're sending a Sunday service reminder to everyone, an SMS alert to a specific small group, or a push notification to your app users. You control the channel, the audience, and the timing, all from one place.

## Composing a Message

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open New Message">
    From the left navigation, go to **Communication > Messages**, then click **New Message** in the top-right corner.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a Channel">
    Select the channel you want to use: **Email**, **SMS**, or **Push Notification**. Each channel has its own composition options and delivery settings.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Your Audience">
    Define who receives the message. You can choose from:
    - **All Members** — sends to every active member in your directory
    - **Specific Group** — targets a small group, ministry team, or volunteer roster
    - **Custom Filter** — build a precise audience using filters like member status, age range, campus, giving history, or attendance record
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write Your Message">
    Enter a **Subject** (email only) and compose your message body. For email, use the rich-text editor to add formatting, images, and links. For SMS and push notifications, keep your copy concise and direct.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Preview Your Message">
    Click **Preview** to see exactly how your message will appear to recipients across desktop and mobile. For email, you can also send a test message to yourself before broadcasting.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send or Schedule">
    When you're satisfied, choose **Send Now** to deliver immediately, or click **Schedule** to pick a future date and time. Scheduled messages appear in the **Drafts & Scheduled** tab until they are sent.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Audience Targeting

Precise targeting ensures the right people receive the right message. ChurchOS lets you filter your audience by:

- **Group membership** — reach members of a specific small group, ministry, choir, or volunteer team
- **Member status** — active members, visitors, inactive members, or custom status tags
- **Age range** — useful for youth ministry, senior programming, or family-focused messages
- **Campus** — if your church has multiple campuses, send campus-specific communications without manual list management

Saved filters can be reused across future messages. To save a filter, build your audience criteria and click **Save Audience** before composing your message.

## Email Templates

Templates save you time when you send recurring messages like weekly bulletins, event invitations, or donation receipts.

**Using an existing template:** When composing an email, click **Insert Template** and choose from your library of saved templates. The template content will populate the editor, where you can make any final adjustments before sending.

**Creating a new template:** Go to **Communication > Templates > New Template**. Design your layout using the rich-text editor, add placeholder variables like `{{first_name}}` or `{{event_date}}` for personalization, and click **Save Template**. Your template will be available to all admin users in your account.

## Message History

Every message you send is logged in **Communication > Messages > Sent**. For each message you can view:

- **Delivery status** — number of recipients, delivered, bounced, and failed
- **Open rate** (email only) — percentage of recipients who opened the message
- **Click rate** (email only) — percentage who clicked at least one link
- **Opt-out count** — members who unsubscribed after receiving the message

Use this data to refine your messaging strategy over time — higher open rates often correlate with clear subject lines and well-timed sends.

## Channel-Specific Tips

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Email">
    - Keep subject lines under **50 characters** so they display fully on mobile devices.
    - Use `{{first_name}}` in the subject or opening line to personalize at scale.
    - Ensure your **Mailjet** or **SendGrid** account is connected under **Settings > Integrations** before sending bulk campaigns to avoid deliverability issues.
    - Always include an unsubscribe link — ChurchOS adds one automatically to every outbound email.
    - Preview on both desktop and mobile using the **Preview** panel before sending.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="SMS">
    - SMS messages are limited to **160 characters** per segment. Messages longer than 160 characters are split into multiple segments and may incur additional SMS credit usage.
    - Keep your call-to-action clear and include a shortened link if directing members to a URL.
    - SMS is ideal for urgent, time-sensitive messages — service cancellations, last-minute location changes, or event-day reminders.
    - Members can reply STOP to opt out. Opt-outs are tracked automatically and those members will not receive future SMS messages.
    - Check your **SMS credit balance** under **Settings > Billing** before large campaigns.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Push Notification">
    - Push notifications are delivered only to members who have installed the **ChurchOS mobile app** and have notifications enabled on their device.
    - Keep the notification title under **65 characters** and the body under **240 characters** for optimal display on both iOS and Android.
    - Push notifications are free on all ChurchOS plans and are great for general announcements, event reminders, and prayer requests.
    - You can deep-link a push notification to a specific section of the app (such as an event page or giving screen) by including a **Deep Link URL** in the composition panel.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Tip>
  Schedule Sunday-morning messages — like service time reminders or worship notes — for **7:00 AM** on Sunday. Members are more likely to engage with a timely, contextual message right before they head out the door than one sent days in advance.
</Tip>
