ChurchOS includes a fully integrated church website builder at no additional cost. Your website is mobile-responsive out of the box, built with SEO best practices so visitors can find you on Google, and deeply connected to the rest of ChurchOS — meaning your events, sermons, and giving page update automatically without you having to manage a separate platform. Whether you're launching a brand-new site or replacing an outdated one, you can have a polished church website live in minutes.

## What's Included

Your ChurchOS website comes with the following pre-built pages, all of which you can customize with your own content:

- **Home Page** — A welcoming landing page with a hero image, your church name and tagline, service times, and quick links.
- **About Page** — Share your church's story, mission, values, and pastoral staff.
- **Sermons Page** — A searchable archive of your sermon recordings, auto-synced from your ChurchOS sermon library.
- **Events Page** — A live calendar of upcoming events, automatically pulled from your ChurchOS Events module.
- **Giving Page** — A dedicated page with an embedded giving form so visitors and members can donate online.
- **Contact Page** — Display your address, phone number, email, and an embedded map so people can find and reach you easily.

## Setting Up Your Website

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Settings > Church Website > Enable">
    In your ChurchOS dashboard, navigate to **Settings**, select **Church Website**, and click **Enable Website**. This provisions your site on a ChurchOS subdomain (e.g., `yourchurch.churchos.com`) so you can start building immediately.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a theme and color scheme">
    Browse the available website themes and select the one that best fits your church's style. Then customize the primary and accent colors to match your brand using the color picker or hex codes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add your church name, tagline, address, and contact info">
    Fill in your church's display name, a short tagline (e.g., "Welcome home — every Sunday at 10am"), your physical address, phone number, and contact email. This information appears in your site footer, contact page, and SEO metadata.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload a banner or hero image">
    Upload a high-quality photo to use as the full-width hero image on your home page. A wide landscape image of your building, sanctuary, or congregation works best (recommended size: 1920×1080px).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add your church logo">
    Upload your church logo to display in the site header and browser tab (favicon). PNG format with a transparent background gives the best results across light and dark backgrounds.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Preview and publish">
    Use the **Preview** button to review how your site looks on desktop and mobile before it goes live. When everything looks good, click **Publish** to make your website publicly accessible.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Add your giving button or a "Give Now" call-to-action directly on your home page hero section. Visitors who land on your site for the first time are far more likely to give if the option is immediately visible rather than buried in a navigation menu.
</Tip>

## Connecting a Custom Domain

Your site starts on a ChurchOS subdomain, but you can connect any domain you own (e.g., `gracechurch.org`) in just a few steps.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Purchase a domain from a registrar">
    If you don't already own a domain, buy one from a registrar such as [GoDaddy](https://www.godaddy.com), [Namecheap](https://www.namecheap.com), or [Squarespace Domains](https://domains.squarespace.com). Choose a name that's short, memorable, and easy to spell.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go to Settings > Website > Custom Domain">
    In your ChurchOS dashboard, navigate to **Settings > Church Website > Custom Domain**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your domain">
    Type your domain name into the field (e.g., `gracechurch.org`) and click **Connect Domain**. ChurchOS will display the DNS records you need to add.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Update your DNS records">
    Log in to your domain registrar and add the DNS records provided by ChurchOS (typically a CNAME or A record pointing to ChurchOS servers). The exact steps vary by registrar — consult your registrar's help center if needed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for DNS propagation">
    DNS changes can take up to **48 hours** to propagate worldwide, though they often take effect within a few hours. Once propagation is complete, your ChurchOS website will be live at your custom domain.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The ChurchOS website builder is **free** and included with your ChurchOS subscription. However, a custom domain (e.g., `yourchurch.org`) must be purchased separately through a third-party domain registrar. ChurchOS does not sell or manage domain registrations.
</Note>

## Adding Pages and Managing Content

### Sermon Archive

Your sermons page is automatically populated from your ChurchOS sermon library. Any sermon you upload or link in **Content > Sermons** will appear on your website's sermon page, complete with title, series, speaker, date, and playback controls. No manual updates required.

### Events Listing

Your events page is live-synced with the **Events** module in ChurchOS. When you create or update an event in ChurchOS, it appears on your website automatically — including the event name, date, time, location, and description. Past events are automatically archived and removed from the public listing.

### Giving Button

Your giving page is pre-built and connected to your ChurchOS giving setup. You can also embed a **Give Now** button on any other page (such as the home page or about page) using the page editor's button component. Funds collected through the website flow directly into your ChurchOS giving dashboard.

## SEO Settings

ChurchOS gives you control over the key SEO fields that help search engines understand and rank your site.

To update your SEO settings, go to **Settings > Church Website > SEO**:

- **Page Titles** — Set a custom title for each page (e.g., "Grace Church — Sunday Services in Austin, TX"). Page titles appear in browser tabs and search results.
- **Meta Descriptions** — Write a 150–160 character summary for each page that describes its content. This is the snippet that appears beneath your link in Google search results.
- **Sitemap** — Enable the auto-generated sitemap so search engines can crawl and index all of your pages. Your sitemap URL will be `yourdomain.com/sitemap.xml`.

Keeping these fields filled in and up to date is one of the most effective ways to help new visitors discover your church online.
